Challenges & Solutions

Problems I Solved

Challenge

Stakeholders across 12 business units had inconsistent visibility into migration progress, leading to duplicated questions and misaligned expectations.

Solution

Built a centralized Power BI dashboard with real-time status, RAG indicators, and drill-down capability by region. Established weekly stakeholder digest emails summarizing key updates.

Challenge

Sprint velocity was unpredictable due to unclear acceptance criteria and scope creep from late-stage requirement changes.

Solution

Introduced a stricter definition of ready with mandatory acceptance criteria templates. Implemented a change request process that required impact assessment before mid-sprint additions.

Challenge

Timezone gaps between NA, EMEA, and APAC teams created handoff delays and blocked progress during off-hours.

Solution

Designed an asynchronous handoff protocol with structured Slack updates and Confluence status pages. Scheduled overlapping sync windows to ensure at least one touchpoint between all regions daily.

Lessons Learned

What I'd Do Again (And What I'd Change)

Invest in visibility infrastructure early

The Power BI dashboard paid dividends throughout the project. Building it in week two instead of week six would have prevented early miscommunications and reduced the "status update" meeting burden.

Definition of Ready is non-negotiable

Stories without clear acceptance criteria create downstream chaos. Holding the line on DoR requirements—even when it slows sprint starts—saves time overall.

Asynchronous communication is a skill, not a workaround

Global teams can't rely on synchronous meetings alone. Designing explicit handoff protocols and written-first communication norms makes distributed work sustainable.
