The core idea

Adoption is a behavior-change problem wearing a software costume.

Configuring the platform is the easy part. The hard part is earning the trust of someone who was already good at the old way — and who, the moment things get hard, has a spreadsheet waiting that always works.

Part 1 · The people

Five people. Five reasons to resist.

You can't move a team by treating "the customer" as one person. Each role weighs the switch differently — and one of them decides everything.

01
The Executive Sponsor
Fears wasted spend. Signs the deal, then vanishes — and the rollout loses its air-cover. Keep them warm with ROI updates.
02
The Champion
Fears looking wrong for backing it. Becomes the lone help-desk and burns out. Make them a hero, not a martyr.
03
The Skeptical Practitioner the linchpin
Fears losing their mastery. They were great at the spreadsheet — now they're a beginner again, so they keep a shadow copy. Win them with one fast win on a task they hate, and the team follows.
04
The Occasional User
Fears friction. One login snag and they email their request the old way — pulling everyone else back out. Give them a 90-second path to their one task.
05
The Admin / Gatekeeper
Fears risk. Security or integration gaps can stall the whole thing indefinitely. Engage them first, not in week six.

The finish line isn't "go-live." It's the day the skeptic deletes their shadow spreadsheet.

Part 2 · Implementation

Don't boil the ocean. Land one win.

Days 0–30

Land one win

Pick one high-pain workflow. Configure only what it needs. Train the champion and skeptic on their real data. Run it once, end to end.

Win: one real workflow lives in the platform.
Days 31–60

Build the habit

Roll out to the team. Target the skeptic directly. Hunt the shadow spreadsheet. Read the health score for the first time.

Win: the platform becomes the default.
Days 61–90

Widen & prove ROI

Add a second workflow now that trust exists. Bring the sponsor a results story tied to why they bought it.

Win: a clear path to renewal.
Part 3 · Customer Success

"Live" is not "adopted."

A health score is only useful if it tracks what actually predicts retention. The deepest signal is simple: has the work moved into the platform, or is it still happening somewhere else?

Weighted: migration depth (30%) · active-user breadth (25%) · engagement depth (20%) · key-feature use (15%) · sentiment (10%). Logins are a vanity metric — every signal here is built to catch reversion.

🟢

Healthy

Across the trough. Workflow migrated, team active, key features in use.

Play: protect & grow. Reinforce ROI; look for expansion.
🟡

At risk

Stuck in the trough. Narrow adoption, or engagement slipping after a strong start.

Play: intervene now. Find the weak signal and target it. This is where CS earns its keep.
🔴

Critical

Sliding back to the old way — and may not even realize it's churning.

Play: escalate. Re-establish the "why," re-run a compressed onboarding around one new win.
